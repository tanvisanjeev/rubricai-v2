COMMUNICATION DOMAIN

Cluster 1: Interview Communication

Indicator 1: Asks clear, open-ended questions
Level 1: Asks 0-1 open-ended questions per 10-min segment; questions vague/confusing (clarification ≥3 times); unclear/leading phrasing; conflate topics; NOT strategically phrased; do NOT invite storytelling
Level 2: Asks 2-3 open-ended questions; sometimes vague (clarification 2-3 times); clear phrasing, no leading; sometimes conflate topics; NOT strategic; no storytelling
Level 3: Asks 4-5 open-ended questions; minimal clarification (1-2 times); clear phrasing; one topic at a time; NOT strategic; no storytelling
Level 4: Asks 6+ open-ended questions; minimal clarification (<1); clear phrasing; one topic; strategic phrasing 3-5 times ("Walk me through...", "Help me understand..."); invites storytelling 2-4 times

Indicator 2: Uses follow-up questions to probe deeper
Level 1: 0-20% follow-up questions; probes 0-25% of key opportunities; moves to next question without probing; no follow-up on follow-up
Level 2: 21-45% follow-up questions; probes 26-50% of opportunities; generic follow-ups; 1-level probing only
Level 3: 46-65% follow-up questions; probes 51-70% of opportunities; references specific statements; 2-level probing
Level 4: 65%+ follow-up questions; probes 70%+ of opportunities; explicitly names what probing; 3+ level probing; uses multiple probing techniques

Indicator 3: Demonstrates active listening
Level 1: 0 active listening behaviors; no paraphrasing/summarizing; clarifying questions show wasn't listening; interrupts ≥3 times
Level 2: 1-2 active listening behaviors; inaccurate paraphrasing; minimal summarizing; basic clarifying questions; interrupts 1-2 times
Level 3: 3-4 active listening behaviors; accurate paraphrasing 3-4 times; summarizes at 1-2 transitions; 1-2 clarifying questions; minimal interruptions
Level 4: 5+ active listening behaviors; paraphrases with interpretation; comprehensive summaries; clarifying questions deepen understanding; no interruptions; uses strategic silence, names patterns, mirrors tone

Indicator 4: Adjusts communication style
Level 1: 0 adaptations; uniform style throughout; does not respond to confusion
Level 2: 1-2 adaptations; reactive only after explicit confusion signals; single-dimension adjustments
Level 3: 3-4 adaptations; responsive when confusion appears; multi-dimension adjustments; align with needs
Level 4: 5+ adaptations; proactive (50%+ before confusion signals); uses meta-communication; consistently enhances effectiveness

Indicator 5: Introduces self and purpose professionally
Level 1: Absent/incomplete/unclear; omits 2+ essential elements; listener cannot determine purpose
Level 2: Includes name/role/purpose but lacks organization; presents as disconnected facts
Level 3: Clearly introduces self/role/purpose in logical order; purpose explicitly linked to context
Level 4: Concise (<45 sec) yet comprehensive; establishes credibility; sets expectations; explains value of participation

Indicator 6: Builds rapport with stakeholders
Level 1: 0 rapport-building behaviors; transactional/disengaged; no acknowledgment; no reference to prior statements; long silences
Level 2: 1-2 rapport-building behaviors; basic acknowledgments; occasional references; generic not specific; minimal emotional responsiveness
Level 3: 3-4 rapport-building behaviors; specific validation; references statements with specificity; acknowledges emotions; consistent verbal encouragers
Level 4: 5+ rapport-building behaviors; finds common ground; integrates stakeholder language; verbal reframing; names patterns; validates expertise

Cluster 2: Communicating Ideas Clearly

Indicator 7: Organizes ideas logically with clear transitions
Level 1: 0 explicit transitions; no organizational structure; logical relationships not signaled
Level 2: 1-2 transitions; basic organization but not previewed; transitions mark shifts but not relationships
Level 3: 3-4 transitions using multiple types; previews structure; 1-2 summaries; signals logical relationships
Level 4: 5+ transitions strategically selected; explicitly names structure; uses meta-commentary; comprehensive synthesis summary

Indicator 8: Connects claims to evidence during communication
Level 1: 0-20% of claims supported; opinion-based assertions; evidence disconnected from claims
Level 2: 21-40% of claims supported; implicit/weak connections; evidence relevant but relationship requires inference
Level 3: 41-60% of claims supported with 1-2 pieces of evidence; explicitly connects evidence to claims with reasoning
Level 4: 60%+ supported; explains not only that evidence supports claim but WHY; addresses quality/limitations/alternatives

Indicator 9: Uses a clear, concise, professional tone
Level 1: 15+ fillers/min; 5+ vague referents per 10 min; overly casual OR jargon-heavy
Level 2: 8-14 fillers/min; 2-4 imprecise instances; professional with minor lapses
Level 3: 3-7 fillers/min; <2 vague referents; consistently professional
Level 4: ≤2 fillers/min; precise and economical language; polished and audience-calibrated

Indicator 10: Adapts message for different audiences
Level 1: Same framing regardless of audience role or context
Level 2: Limited surface-level adjustments; core framing unchanged
Level 3: Adjusts framing/terminology/emphasis based on audience role and knowledge
Level 4: Strategically reframes by anticipating information needs, decision contexts, and constraints

CRITICAL THINKING DOMAIN

Cluster 1: Information Analysis

Indicator 11: Asks clear and targeted questions to explore issues
Level 1: 0-1 task-relevant questions; broad/generic/unclear; no follow-up; no logical sequence
Level 2: 2-3 relevant questions; lack focus or redundant; 1-2 reactive follow-ups; limited logical progression
Level 3: 4-5 relevant clearly worded questions; 3-4 follow-ups (general to specific); logical inquiry path; avoids redundancy
Level 4: 6+ high-value precisely targeted questions; 5+ follow-ups testing understanding; asks meta-questions; prioritizes high-leverage questions

Indicator 12: Identifies assumptions, gaps, or contradictions
Level 1: Identifies 0; overlooks clear contradictions
Level 2: Identifies 1-2 vague/incomplete gaps; notices contradictions when pointed out; doesn't explain impact
Level 3: Identifies 2-3 specific assumptions/contradictions; uses targeted questions to resolve; explains why it matters
Level 4: Identifies 4+; distinguishes critical from minor gaps; tests assumptions; clarifies implications; identifies what would resolve uncertainties

Indicator 13: Evaluates relevance of information
Level 1: 0 explicit references to earlier information; treats each response as isolated; no adjustments
Level 2: 1-2 references with 50%+ errors; adjusts 1-2 times then reverts; no quality evaluation
Level 3: 3-4 accurate references connecting information; adjusts questioning 3-5 times; evaluates information quality
Level 4: 5+ accurate references; adjusts fluidly throughout; explicitly evaluates information 3+ times (importance, confidence, sufficiency)

Indicator 14: Breaks down complex problems
Level 1: Describes globally; no distinct components; unorganized list
Level 2: 1-2 components, omits key elements; basic list; no relationships explained
Level 3: 3-4 components with clear framework; explains how 2-3 relate; no critical gaps
Level 4: 5+ coherent components; optimized structure; prioritizes by impact; shows dependencies; acknowledges limitations

Cluster 2: Insight Synthesis

Indicator 15: Identifies relationships across data points
Level 1: 0 connections; describes each point separately
Level 2: 1-2 basic connections; identifies similarity but not mechanism
Level 3: 3-4 connections with mechanisms/causation; uses connections to build understanding
Level 4: 5+ integrated connections; identifies interaction patterns; maps causal chains; distinguishes direct vs indirect

Indicator 16: Recognizes patterns, themes, and root causes
Level 1: 0 patterns; purely descriptive; no distinction between surface and underlying
Level 2: 1-2 emerging patterns with basic labeling; incomplete descriptions; symptoms confused with causes
Level 3: 3-4 legitimate patterns with clear labels; supports each with examples from multiple sources; distinguishes symptoms from 1-2 root causes
Level 4: 5+ patterns including surface and deep themes; multiple examples with significance explained; root cause with rationale; distinguishes frequency vs intensity; handles contradictions

Indicator 17: Synthesizes evidence into insights
Level 1: 0 insights; only restates facts; no causal reasoning; no implications
Level 2: 1-2 descriptive patterns; groups observations but no WHY; incomplete causal statements; generic implications
Level 3: 2-3 thematic insights with causal reasoning; 1-level causal explanation; specific implications tied to context
Level 4: 4+ systemic insights; multi-level causal reasoning; root cause vs symptoms; maps systemic connections; addresses contradictions; non-obvious understanding; specific actionable prioritized implications

Indicator 18: Organizes insights with clear logical structure
Level 1: Disconnected list; no grouping; no clear structure
Level 2: Attempts grouping but logic unclear; 2-3 insights without explained relationships; basic structure
Level 3: Clear framework explicitly named; explains how insights relate; logical sequence with transitions
Level 4: Hierarchical/relational structure; states organizing principle upfront; shows insights work together; prioritizes by importance

Cluster 3: Decision Framing and Evidence-Based Judgment

Indicator 19: Clearly frames the decision or direction
Level 1: No clear decision stated; problem and decision blended
Level 2: Partial articulation; scope/criteria/alternatives vague; no decision criteria
Level 3: Clear decision question/options; indicates criteria/constraints; complete enough to understand
Level 4: Precise decision space with options/constraints/criteria; distinguishes short vs long term; clarifies scope; sequences related decisions

Indicator 20: Connects ideas and recommendations to evidence
Level 1: 0 connections to evidence; preference-based reasoning
Level 2: Cites evidence 1-2 times weakly; ≤40% of recommendations connected
Level 3: 60-80% of recommendations supported with specific evidence; explicit connection statements
Level 4: 80%+ supported with multiple converging evidence; reasoning chain explicit; contrasts alternatives; acknowledges evidence limits

Indicator 21: Addresses uncertainty, limitations, trade-offs
Level 1: Treats info as certain; no caveats; defensive when challenged
Level 2: Vaguely acknowledges uncertainty; doesn't specify; mentions alternatives only when asked
Level 3: Identifies 2-3 specific uncertainties/trade-offs; explains impact; recognizes alternative explanations
Level 4: Identifies 4+ uncertainties; explains impact; weighs alternatives with evidence; suggests ways to reduce uncertainty; distinguishes critical vs minor unknowns

Indicator 22: Provides logical, feasible, client-aligned recommendation
Level 1: No clear recommendation OR internally inconsistent/infeasible/unrelated
Level 2: Generic recommendation; partially misaligned with constraints; lacks specificity
Level 3: Specific actionable recommendation addressing core problem; feasible given constraints; explains why it addresses need
Level 4: Focused recommendation tailored to context; follows from prior analysis; addresses feasibility; scopes appropriately; anticipates implementation

PROFESSIONAL AGENCY DOMAIN

Cluster 1: Professional Self-Efficacy

Indicator 23: Communicates with clarity and presence
Level 1: Uncertain tone/mumbles; fragmented responses; no structure; requires 3+ prompts
Level 2: Audible but lacks projection; 10+ fillers/min; inconsistent structure; requires 1-2 prompts
Level 3: Clear audible voice; 3-7 fillers/min; logical structure; no prompts needed
Level 4: Natural projection; ≤2 fillers/min; clear structure with implication; maintains composure when challenged

Indicator 24: Asks questions and seeks clarification confidently
Level 1: 0 clarification instances; proceeds despite confusion; avoids engagement
Level 2: 1-2 clarifications after prolonged struggle; vague questions; hesitant/apologetic tone
Level 3: 3-4 specific clarifications; task-relevant and targeted; straightforward without apology
Level 4: 5+ strategic clarifications; distinguishes types of information needed; proposes answer and checks; asks about assumptions

Indicator 25: Demonstrates comfort in formal/unfamiliar environments
Level 1: 0 composure instances; high anxiety; voice trembling ≥3 times; withdraws; requires 3+ reassurances
Level 2: 1-2 engagement instances; moderate anxiety; performance deteriorates under pressure; recovers with 1-2 encouragements
Level 3: 3-5 engagement instances; controlled vocal patterns; recovers within 10-15 seconds; continues without withdrawal
Level 4: 5+ engagement instances; vocal qualities consistent; uses composure strategies; recovers in ≤5 seconds; acknowledges discomfort without losing effectiveness

Indicator 26: Expresses calibrated confidence
Level 1: Pre-task ≥2 levels miscalibrated; post-task also inaccurate; cannot identify strengths; fixed mindset
Level 2: 1-2 levels miscalibrated; limited improvement post-task; vague strengths without examples
Level 3: Within 0.5-1 level calibrated; accurate post-task; 2-3 specific strengths with examples; growth language
Level 4: Within 0.5 levels; identifies 3-4 strengths and 2+ growth areas with evidence; links confidence to evidence; nuanced self-awareness

Cluster 2: Initiative and Action-Taking

Indicator 27: Begins tasks without excessive reassurance
Level 1: Requires 3+ prompts; never volunteers; waits passively
Level 2: Requires 1-2 prompts; occasionally volunteers (1-2 instances); doesn't drive conversation
Level 3: Begins without prompting; volunteers 3-5 ideas/questions; takes responsibility for advancing
Level 4: Immediately engages; volunteers 6-8; explicitly owns agenda; proposes next steps; takes meta-initiative

Indicator 28: Takes purposeful action under ambiguity
Level 1: 0 instances of action framing; avoidance or vague statements
Level 2: 1-2 instances but reactive/nonspecific
Level 3: 3+ instances; concrete steps despite incomplete information; makes assumptions explicit
Level 4: Consistently structured decision-making; identifies uncertainty → action → contingency; frames obstacles as solvable

Indicator 29: Identifies needed resources and concrete steps
Level 1: 0 resource identification; doesn't recognize missing information
Level 2: 1-2 vague/passive resource awareness; no clear next step
Level 3: 3+ specific resource identification; names stakeholders/tools; articulates how to access
Level 4: Consistently identifies appropriate resources; efficient prioritized steps; links to advancing task

Indicator 30: Demonstrates persistence and ownership
Level 1: 0 persistence; disengages when difficult; shifts responsibility
Level 2: 1-2 instances; short-lived; dependent on encouragement; limited quality attention
Level 3: 3+ sustained engagement; continues through difficulty; articulates steps for quality
Level 4: Consistently demonstrates ownership; frames quality as personal responsibility; anticipates challenges

Cluster 3: Growth-Oriented Mindset

Indicator 31: Interprets feedback as information for improvement
Level 1: Defensive to challenges; describes feedback as personal criticism; no learning identified
Level 2: Accepts without defensiveness but minimal engagement; vague interpretation; 1 use without explanation
Level 3: Responds constructively; reframes 2-3 feedback instances as improvement info; links to concrete actions
Level 4: Actively engages; systematic causal improvement description; prioritizes feedback by impact; shows meta-awareness

Indicator 32: Reflects on learning with concrete examples
Level 1: Cannot identify improvement examples; static ability terms only
Level 2: 1-2 general areas without specifics; vague temporal comparison; cannot name contributors
Level 3: 3-4 specific skills with before-after comparison; names contributors; concrete example with details
Level 4: 5+ skills with detailed evidence; explains what/why/evidence for each; identifies improvement patterns; quantifies growth

Indicator 33: Responds to setbacks by adjusting strategies
Level 1: Becomes discouraged; attributes to fixed ability; disengages; interprets struggle as inability
Level 2: Brief discouragement; tries same approach again; mixed attribution; needs external support
Level 3: Recovers in 3-5 seconds; tries different approach; 2-3 specific strategy adjustments; controllable attributions
Level 4: Reframes setbacks as information; 2-3 different approaches; detailed setback→adjustment→outcome narrative; meta-awareness of resilience growth

Indicator 34: Demonstrates openness to experimentation
Level 1: 0 instances; avoids change; prefers familiar
Level 2: 1-2 instances; unclear rationale
Level 3: 3+ intentional instances; names strategy, reason, and outcome
Level 4: Consistently experiments; compares strategies; evaluates results; generalizes lessons
